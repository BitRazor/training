How to test
Tempo IS the exercise. Every rep — ramps included — at 3 s down / 3 s up (calves 3-3-3). A fast rep gives a number ~20–30% too heavy and miscalibrates the whole block. Use the tempo button below.
Warm up first. 3–5 min backward sled or backward treadmill, then World's Greatest Stretch · inchworm · quadruped step-through · lateral drop lunge · eyes-closed balance — then ramp the first lift.
1
Ramp. Light → 3–4 reps at tempo → rest ~1 min → add weight → again. 2–3 steps, until it feels like "I could do ~15–18 of these."
2
Set 1 = the test. That weight, to failure, strict tempo. Failure = the rep slows past tempo or form breaks. Stop there — no ugly grinding.
3
Read the reps (the app does this for you): 13–17 → calibrated, that's the 15RM · 18+ → +5–10% next set · ≤11 → −10% next set. Sets 2–3 at the corrected weight.
Gap-Closure lifts are NOT tested to failure. Find the weight where rep 13–14 is hard but you're sure 1–2 more were there (1–2 RIR). Log that.
In doubt between two weights → take the lighter. Undershooting costs nothing in week 1; overshooting strains the tendon.
Rest 2.5 min Heavy · 1.5 min Gap-Closure · ~45 s semi-warm-up. Unilateral: left, then right, then rest = one set.
Pain rule (even in week 1): ≤3/10 that's gone by next morning = fine. Climbing through a set, or worse next morning = drop that load.
